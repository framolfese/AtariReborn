ATARI

Among the most important products of the company we mention the domestic consoles such as the Atari 2600 (VCS); a series of eight-bit computers (Atari 400, 800, 600 and 800XL, 65 e130XE), successful in the '80s; Atari took part in the revolution of 16/32 bit computers first with Atari ST and then with Atari Falcon; built the revolutionary (for its time) Atari Jaguar 64-bit console, unfortunately proved a failure and launched Atari Lynx, the first color portable console.

SPACE INVADERS

There is a widespread urban legend according to which, due to the enormous success of the game, in Japan there would have been a significant lack of 100 Yen coins, the amount required to play Space Invaders, and to solve the problem the Japanese government was forced to quadruple the emissions of those coins.

SNAKE

The first version designed for home computers, called Worm, was developed in 1979 by F. Seger (Germany) on the TRS-80 computer, followed closely by a version for the Commodore VIC-20. A version of Blockade called Hustler was then developed by the Milton Bradley Company for the TI-99 / 4A in 1980.

PACMAN

Pac-Man is a video game designed by Toru Iwatani and produced by Namco in 1980 in the arcade format. In the West it was licensed by Midway Games. Immediately acquired great popularity and, in the following years, under the label Namco have been published several versions for almost all consoles and computers, preserving until today its reputation as a classic video games.

DONKEY KONG

Donkey Kong is an arcade video game platform developed by Nintendo and released in arcades in 1981. It was later converted for numerous types of computers and consoles, as well as Game & Watch. In the game appear for the first time the famous characters Mario (to know more about the role of Atari in the choice of the name, click on the button History at the top) and Donkey Kong.
